The Problem We Solved
Why This Process Exists
Before this framework, cost management was left entirely to individual team members with no standardised process. This created recurring problems that cost us time, money and accuracy.
📁

Multiple Conflicting Files

Different versions of the same data existed across multiple files. Nobody knew which was current, leading to costs calculated on wrong prices.

🔀

No Standard Format

Every barman and chef built their cost files differently. Impossible to compare or audit across outlets.

⚠️

Prices Not Cross-Checked

Ingredient prices were entered manually from memory without systematic verification. Errors went undetected for months.

🔁

Work Done Multiple Times

Without a clear sequence, cost files were built before the market list was finalised, forcing the entire work to be repeated.

📏

Unit of Measure Confusion

The same ingredient appeared with different unit references across different files. Grams vs kilograms, bottles vs ml.

🔍

No Central Oversight

Administration had no visibility into how costs were calculated. No mechanism to verify, audit or control the process.

✓

The solution: A single sequential process, standardised templates, fixed roles, and a live database that tracks every price change automatically. This document and the tools below are that solution.

The Method
The Four-Phase Process
Every menu launch must follow this exact sequence. No phase can be skipped. No phase can start before the previous one is complete.
1
Phase One
Testing
Drinks and dishes are tested. Draft recipes with estimated costs are created.
2
Phase Two
Menu Approval
Management reviews and approves the final menu. Only approved items proceed.
3
Phase Three
Market List
The Master Market List is built or updated with all raw materials needed.
4
Phase Four
Cost Files
Chef and barman finalise official cost files using the approved Market List.
Phase by Phase
Detailed Phase Instructions
Each phase has specific steps, responsibilities and deliverables. Expand each phase to read the full instructions.
1
Testing Phase — Drink & Food Tests
Who: Barman / Chef | Output: Draft recipe sheets with estimated costs
▶
  • 1
    Create and test all candidate recipes

    The barman or chef develops all candidate drinks or dishes. Each recipe must be documented with exact ingredient quantities (in grams or ml) and the preparation method. Nothing stays in the team member's head — it must all be written down.

  • 2
    Build draft cost sheets using the standard template

    Using the official template provided by administration, each recipe is entered with ingredient quantities and estimated prices. At this stage prices are approximate — they will be confirmed in Phase 4 against the Market List.

  • 3
    Submit all draft cost sheets to administration

    The complete set of draft recipes and cost sheets is submitted to the administration team before any menu decision is made. No recipe is considered complete until submitted in the correct format.

✓

Must: All draft cost sheets must use the official standardised template. Custom-built spreadsheets are not accepted.

✗

Never: Do not build cost files after the menu has been approved. The draft must exist before approval.

2
Menu Approval — Selection of Final Items
Who: Management / Administration | Output: Approved menu list
▶
  • 1
    Review all submitted draft cost sheets

    Management and administration review all submitted recipes and draft costs. Focus is on menu selection — which items to approve — not on the precision of costs (that comes in Phase 4).

  • 2
    Approve or reject each item

    Each recipe is marked as Approved or Not Approved. Only approved items proceed. Rejected items are archived.

  • 3
    Issue the approved menu list

    A final approved menu list is communicated in writing to the barman/chef and the administration team. This document marks the start of Phase 3.

✓

Must: Menu approval must be issued in writing. Verbal approvals are not valid.

✗

Never: Do not start building the Market List before the approved menu list has been issued.

3
Market List — Master Raw Materials File
Who: Administration / Accounting | Output: Complete, verified Master Market List
▶
  • 1
    Identify all raw materials required

    Based on the approved menu list, compile every ingredient needed across all recipes. This becomes the scope of the Market List for this menu cycle.

  • 2
    Verify prices with suppliers

    For each ingredient, confirm the current purchase price directly with the supplier. Do not use prices from memory or old files. Prices must be verified at the time of the Market List build.

  • 3
    Enter all items in the live Market List database

    Using the Market List tool in this platform, enter every raw material with: exact product name, category, brand, supplier, unit, quantity per pack and purchase price. All price-per-unit calculations happen automatically.

  • 4
    Administration validates and marks all items as Verified

    Once complete, the administration team reviews each entry and marks it as VERIFIED with their name and date. The Verification Panel tracks all pending items automatically.

✓

Must: There is ONE Master Market List. All outlets reference the same database. No local copies or separate files are permitted.

✗

Never: Do not allow the barman or chef to add or modify prices in the Market List. It is owned exclusively by administration.

4
Cost File Finalisation — Official Drink & Food Costs
Who: Barman / Chef (supervised by Administration) | Output: Approved, verified cost files
▶
  • 1
    Barman/Chef receives the validated Market List and standard templates

    Administration provides the validated Market List and the official template. The barman/chef must use these files exactly as provided — no modifications to the template structure are permitted.

  • 2
    Complete the cost files using approved Market List prices only

    Every ingredient name, unit and price must match the Market List exactly. If an ingredient is not in the Market List, work must stop and the issue must be reported to administration.

  • 3
    Submit completed cost files to administration for audit

    All completed cost files are submitted to administration. Do not consider the work done until this submission is confirmed received.

  • 4
    Administration performs the cross-check audit

    Administration verifies every ingredient price and unit against the Market List. Any discrepancy is flagged and sent back for correction. This step repeats until zero discrepancies remain.

  • 5
    Final approval — Status set to APPROVED

    Once the audit passes with zero issues, administration marks the cost files as officially Approved. Only at this point are the costs valid for operational and financial reporting.

✓

Must: If an ingredient is missing from the Market List, the barman/chef must stop and notify administration immediately. They must not invent or estimate a price.

✗

Never: Cost files are never final until they carry the formal APPROVED status from administration. Submitted does not mean Approved.

Non-Negotiable
The Golden Rules
These rules apply to every person, every outlet, every menu cycle. They are not guidelines — they are requirements.

Always — Must Do

  • Follow the four phases in exact sequence — never skip or reorder
  • Use the official standardised templates for all cost work
  • Reference only the validated Master Market List for ingredient prices
  • Submit cost files to administration before considering work complete
  • Report missing Market List ingredients to administration immediately
  • Apply prices using the correct unit of measure
  • Wait for written Approved status before treating a cost file as final
  • Update the Market List whenever a supplier price changes

Never — Must Not Do

  • Never start cost files after the menu is approved — drafts must exist first
  • Never build or edit the Market List without administration authorisation
  • Never use prices from memory, old files or personal estimates
  • Never modify the structure of an official template
  • Never create a personal copy of any official file
  • Never mix units across the same ingredient
  • Never treat a submitted cost file as approved — only admin can approve
  • Never proceed past a missing ingredient — stop and report to admin
